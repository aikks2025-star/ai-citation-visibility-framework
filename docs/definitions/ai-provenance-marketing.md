# AI Provenance in Marketing

**Definition:** AI provenance in marketing is the verifiable trail behind a marketing claim: who wrote it, what primary source supports it, when it was last updated, and how it was reviewed.

This extends the logic of media provenance into claim-level marketing trust. It does not claim that C2PA defines marketing claim provenance; C2PA is a useful reference point for digital content provenance.

## Why it matters

AI systems prefer sources they can verify. Named authorship, primary sources, dated updates, review ownership, and clear evidence chains make a marketing page easier to trust and cite.

## Measurement method

Evaluate author identity, source proximity, update dates, citations, external corroboration, schema, and whether key claims can be traced to public evidence.
